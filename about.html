About Vincent van Gogh

The Artist's Life

Vincent Willem van Gogh was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter who posthumously became one of the most famous and influential figures in Western art history. In a decade, he created about 2,100 artworks, including around 860 oil paintings, most of which date from the last two years of his life. They include landscapes, still lifes, portraits and self-portraits, and are characterised by bold colours and dramatic, impulsive and expressive brushwork that contributed to the foundations of modern art.

Born into an upper-middle-class family, Van Gogh drew as a child and was serious, quiet, and thoughtful. As a young man, he worked as an art dealer, often traveling, but became depressed after he was transferred to London. He turned to religion and spent time as a Protestant missionary in southern Belgium. He drifted in ill health and solitude before taking up painting in 1881, having moved back home with his parents.

Early Life

Vincent van Gogh was born on March 30, 1853, in Zundert, Netherlands. His early life was marked by a deep appreciation for art and nature, though he wouldn't begin his artistic career until later in life.

Artistic Journey

Van Gogh's artistic career began relatively late in his life, but his passion and dedication led to the creation of over 2,100 artworks, including around 850 paintings, in just over a decade.
