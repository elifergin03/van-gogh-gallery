Sunflowers (1888)

Oil on canvas, National Gallery, London

Van Gogh painted several versions of Sunflowers in Arles, France, in 1888 and 1889. These paintings are among his most famous and are known for their vibrant colors and expressive brushwork.

Café Terrace at Night (1888)

Oil on canvas, Kröller-Müller Museum

This painting depicts a café in Arles, France, at night. It is one of the first paintings in which Van Gogh used a starry sky as a background, a theme he would revisit in The Starry Night.

The Bedroom (1888)

Oil on canvas, Van Gogh Museum, Amsterdam

This painting depicts Van Gogh's bedroom in Arles, France. It is one of three versions of the painting, each with slight variations in color and detail.

Irises (1889)

Oil on canvas, J. Paul Getty Museum, Los Angeles

Van Gogh painted Irises while he was staying at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, France. The painting is known for its vibrant colors and dynamic composition.

The Yellow House (1888)

Oil on canvas, Van Gogh Museum, Amsterdam

This painting depicts the house in Arles, France, where Van Gogh lived and worked for a time. The house is known for its bright yellow color, which Van Gogh used to symbolize hope and friendship.

Self Portrait (1889)

Oil on canvas, Musée d'Orsay, Paris

This self-portrait is one of many that Van Gogh painted during his lifetime. It is known for its intense gaze and vibrant colors, which reflect Van Gogh's emotional state at the time.

Almond Blossoms (1890)

Oil on canvas, Van Gogh Museum, Amsterdam

This painting was created to celebrate the birth of Van Gogh's nephew, Vincent Willem. The almond blossoms symbolize new life and hope, and the painting is known for its delicate beauty and vibrant colors.

The Potato Eaters (1885)

Oil on canvas, Van Gogh Museum, Amsterdam

This painting depicts a group of peasants eating potatoes by lamplight. It is one of Van Gogh's early works and is known for its dark, earthy tones and expressive brushwork.
